Swarthmore Christian Fellowship Charter

Last revised: April, 1995 by Carl Heiberg '96

I. Name of Organization: Swarthmore Christian Fellowship

II. Purpose/Goals: Swarthmore Christian Fellowship is a community of students who commit to follow Jesus Christ as their Lord and Savior. They welcome all interested in learning more about Jesus Christ and the Bible. The Fellowship believes that Jesus is the son of God and in the Divine inspiration and authority of the Bible; they seek to share the Good News of Jesus Christ with the campus.

III. Practice:
  • LARGE GROUP: The entire Fellowship meets in Bond on Friday at 7:30 p.m. In addition to singing and prayer, Fellowship meetings sometimes feature guest speakers who discuss various issues relating to the Christian faith. The meetings can follow a more informal format, including sharing experiences, discussing issues, and doing fun activities.
  • SMALL GROUPS: Smaller groups gather in dorms during the week, taking the time out from academics to study the Bible.
  • PRAYER MEETINGS: SCF schedules daily all-campus prayer meetings (usually Bond Basement) and prayer meetings in dorms (student's rooms).
  • INVESTIGATIVE BIBLE STUDIES: Students who want to find out more about Jesus Christ may want to join an Investigative Bible Study group to examine and discuss the Scriptures.
  • ALL CAMPUS: The Fellowship sponsors several all-college events throughout the year, including the Ice Cream Social and the Easter Sunrise Service, and hosts several all-campus speakers. We also have a small but growing library of literature relating to the Christian Faith open for use by the college community in the Community Resource Center (Parrish 2nd).
  • COMMUNICATION/ PUBLICITY: SCF uses electronic mail to send a weekly newsletter to all who want to receive information regarding SCF activities and other SCF concerns. SCF activities and other information concerning the organization are posted on the Swarthmore Christian Fellowship bulletin board located at the east end of Parrish (south wall).
IV. Constituents: Everyone is welcome to attend our meetings, receive the weekly newsletter and be on our mailing lists (voice mail, campus mail, electronic mail) regardless of sex, race, color, age, religious background or personal beliefs, national origin, sexual preference or handicap. All are especially welcome to attend Large Group and other all-campus events. Swarthmore Christian Fellowship presently has about 50 active members, and over 120 Swarthmore students are on our mailing list.

V. Leadership: Decisions will be made by the team of leaders nominated by the entire fellowship and selected by the graduating seniors.

VI. Budget: SCF expenses are expected to be $150 to $200 per month from transportation, refreshments for the Large Group Meeting and some all-campus events, scholarships to retreats and conferences, bibles, and other books for the library.

VII. Role: SCF is affiliated with Inter-Varsity Christian Fellowship, a national, inter-denominational organization which is part of the International Fellowship of Evangelical Students. The Fellowship is not associated with any Christian denomination. Although there are other Christian organizations on campus, the aforementioned beliefs listed under Part I (Purpose/Goals) make us unique. A faith rooted in the foundational beliefs regarding the Bible and Jesus and sharing the Good News with the campus are are essential to many members of the college community who become involved in Swarthmore Christian Fellowship.
